Pizza

Description

Pizza is a savory dish of Italian origin consisting of a usually round, flattened base of leavened wheat-based dough topped with tomatoes, cheese, and often various other ingredients (such as anchovies, mushrooms, onions, olives, vegetables, meat, etc.), which is then baked at a high temperature, traditionally in a wood-fired oven. A small pizza is sometimes called a pizzetta.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 cups warm water (110 degrees F/45 degrees C)
  • 2 1/4 teaspoons active dry yeast
  • 3 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/2 cup pizza sauce
  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • Toppings of your choice (pepperoni, mushrooms, bell peppers, etc.)

Steps

  1. In a small bowl, dissolve yeast and sugar in warm water. Let stand until creamy, about 10 minutes.
  2. In a large bowl, combine flour and salt. Make a well in the center and add the yeast mixture and olive oil. Mix until a dough forms.
  3. Turn the dough onto a floured surface and knead until smooth and elastic, about 6-8 minutes. Place in a greased bowl, cover, and let rise in a warm place until doubled in size, about 1 hour.
  4. Preheat oven to 475 degrees F (245 degrees C). Punch down the dough and divide it into two equal pieces. Roll out each piece into a circle and place on greased pizza pans.
  5. Spread pizza sauce over the dough, leaving a small border around the edges. Sprinkle with mozzarella cheese and add your desired toppings.
  6. Bake in the preheated oven until the crust is golden brown and the cheese is bubbly, about 12-15 minutes.
